What is Artificial Intelligence?

AI is software that learns patterns from millions of examples and uses those patterns to make decisions, generate content, or answer questions — without being given specific rules for every situation.

💬

Language AI

Reads, writes, explains, answers

Examples: Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity.

These AI models are trained on huge amounts of text. They predict the most useful next word, one at a time, until they've answered your question.

Use for: essays, summaries, tutoring, coding, analysis.
🎨

Image AI

Generates & edits images from text

Examples: Adobe Firefly, DALL-E, Midjourney, Ideogram.

These models learn what objects look like from billions of images. You describe what you want, and it generates something that matches your description.

Use for: presentations, social media, creative projects, design.
🎬

Video AI

Creates short video clips from prompts

Examples: Runway ML, Kling AI, Sora, Pika Labs.

Newer and still imperfect — hands, faces, and physics sometimes look wrong. But improving fast.

Use for: explainer videos, creative projects, content creation, presentations.
The best analogy: AI is like an incredibly fast pattern-matcher. It hasn't "understood" anything — it's found the statistical patterns in vast amounts of human knowledge and learned to produce outputs that follow those patterns. This is why it can be brilliant and confidently wrong at the same time.
⚡ Prompt Builder — Try It
❌ vs ✅ Examples
❌ Weak Prompt

"Tell me about cybersecurity"

Too vague. AI will give a generic 1000-word essay that probably doesn't match what you needed.

✅ Strong Prompt

"You are a cybersecurity tutor. List the 5 most common ways hackers attack systems, in simple terms, suitable for a 1st-year IT student."

Specific, scoped, with role + format + audience = targeted, useful answer.

❌ Weak Prompt

"Help me with my essay"

AI has no idea what topic, what level, what's already written, or what kind of help is needed.

✅ Strong Prompt

"Here is my introduction paragraph. Give me 3 specific suggestions to make the argument stronger. Do not rewrite it — just give bullet point feedback."

Clear constraints, specific output format, preserves student's own work.

📚
Summarise Content

Paste a chapter or article. Use these prompts:

  • "Summarise this in 5 key points"
  • "What are the 3 most important ideas here?"
  • "Give me a one-paragraph summary for revision"
  • "What questions might come up in an exam on this?"
🧪
Self-Testing & Quizzes

Use AI as your personal quiz master:

  • "Create 10 multiple choice questions on [topic]"
  • "Quiz me on [chapter]. Ask one question at a time."
  • "I answered X. Was I right? Explain."
  • "What are common mistakes students make on this topic?"
✍️
Essay & Writing Help

Use AI as a writing coach, not a ghostwriter:

  • "Give feedback on this paragraph — don't rewrite it"
  • "Does my argument flow logically? What's missing?"
  • "Suggest 3 ways to make my intro more compelling"
  • "What counterarguments should I address in my essay?"
🔍
Research & Explanation

Understand hard topics faster:

  • "Explain [concept] like I've never studied it"
  • "Give me a real-world example of [theory]"
  • "Compare and contrast X and Y in a simple table"
  • "What are the pros and cons of [approach]?"
💻
Coding & Tech Help

For CS/IT students:

  • "Explain what this code does line by line"
  • "Why is my code giving this error? [paste code]"
  • "Write a simple Python function that does X"
  • "What's the difference between X and Y in [language]?"
⚠️
What AI Can't Do

Always watch out for these limitations:

  • AI can invent fake citations — always verify
  • AI can be confidently wrong about facts & dates
  • AI doesn't know recent events (knowledge cutoff)
  • Submitting AI work as your own = academic dishonesty

💡 Tips for Better AI Video Prompts

01
Describe the camera: "close-up shot", "wide angle", "slow motion", "cinematic tracking shot"
02
Set the scene: lighting, time of day, location. "Golden hour lighting in a Lagos market"
03
Keep it short: AI video works best for 3–8 second clips with one clear action
04
Avoid complex physics: hands, running crowds, and water are hard. Faces in close-up are often imperfect.
05
Chain clips: Create multiple short clips and stitch them in CapCut or any video editor for a longer piece.
🎭

Hallucinations

AI can state false information confidently. It doesn't "know" anything — it predicts likely words. Always verify facts from real sources.

📋

Academic Integrity

Submitting AI-written work as your own is dishonest. Use AI as a learning aid — to understand, not to replace your thinking.

🔒

Privacy

Your prompts may be stored or used for training. Never share passwords, personal ID, or sensitive private information with AI tools.

⚖️

Bias

AI reflects biases in the data it was trained on. It can reproduce cultural, racial, and gender stereotypes. Think critically about its outputs.
